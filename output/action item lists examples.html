What Is an Action Item?

Many people write down tasks or chores they need to complete. The practice helps keep them organized. The formal name for each specific task is an action item. Creating a ''to do'' or action item list helps people organize their tasks and meet their personal goals. Everyone can benefit from creating an action item list, but they are especially useful in business.

Action Item Meaning: Example

David works for a Fortune 500 company as a busy Vice President with broad responsibilities. David supervises a department that opens new business branches in a three-state area. A diverse group of employees is involved in the process. Opening a new branch requires many steps. David knows that delegating tasks and following through on completion is key to the success of the many simultaneous projects in the three-state area the department serves. David develops specific action items that are necessary for every project.

The action item definition involves breaking down complex projects into specific, measurable tasks that can be assigned to various employees involved in a project. David has discovered that action item lists help ensure the project can be completed in a timely way. Stakeholders have visibility into project progress at any given time by viewing the action item list. David can calculate the % of the project that is completed and provide estimated completion dates for the specific projects. The ability to manage the projects while keeping upper-level management informed has been key to David's professional success. An action item list is a major tool for project management success.


Successful project management requires careful planning.

Creating Action Item Lists

Action item lists are useful in many situations, but especially for complex projects with many tasks. Using the previous example, David meets with managers to break down work into action items for all new projects. Experience has shown the team that managers must brainstorm and plan the steps as they develop specific action items needed to complete the project. Many projects are similar, and the team simply updates previous action item lists with information specific to the new project. It's difficult to develop the action item list for projects that are different from previous projects. David and the management team have found that the process of identifying the individual tasks required for the action items helps them understand the scope of the project and identify potential problems.

As part of the process, the management team takes the additional step of identifying key employees to complete individual action items. Assigning specific action items to specific employees helps David's management team hold employees accountable for their assigned tasks. This helps keep the project moving forward on time.

How to Create Actionable Items

It's most effective to write complete sentences describing each specific task, including details, due dates, and steps to complete. The action items are listed in sequential order. The list should include the priority of the task, meaning the importance of the task. If high-priority tasks aren't completed on time, it can derail the project and prevent completion in the desired time frame.

Action items answer the following questions:

  • WHAT is the task to be completed? Complete sentences, written as instructions, with verbs are most effective. The task should include specific information necessary to complete the task. Risks or possible issues should be included.
  • WHO is assigned to complete the task and WHO is responsible for monitoring completion? Specific employees need to be accountable for the completion of their assigned tasks. The management team should logically assign tasks to employees with the skills best suited to the task. Specific managers monitor progress and follow up with the assigned employees. Employees should be aware of which manager they will report to about progress or problems.
  • WHEN is the due date for task completion? Completion dates allow managers to follow up with employees about task status. Specific dates and times should be included.

David and the management team know that spending the time to set up detailed, complete action item lists for every project they're responsible for helps save time and meet project goals.

Tracking Action Items

Like most successful project managers, David and the management team utilize an action item list tracker once the tasks have been developed and organized. Action item trackers come in many formats. Spreadsheets like Excel or Google Sheets are easy to set up. Specific project management software provides a more sophisticated solution. Cloud storage allows all stakeholders involved in the project to have real-time access to the tracker. Specific details include:

  • All members of the team should be able to access the tracker at all times to monitor progress.
  • Employees responsible for specific action items have access to due dates, resources needed, and special instructions, such as contact people who can provide information or help troubleshoot problems.
  • Many projects involve staff in different locations. Methods of communication between all staff should be outlined. Email, chat or texting, phone calls, and face-to-face or virtual meetings are all effective.
  • Some action items will be completed in sequential order. Some action items must be completed before the next action item can be started. Other tasks will be completed simultaneously. The tracking system should include this information.
  • As employees complete tasks, they notate details on the tracker.
  • Team leaders and management monitor the tracker and update the completion status.

Successful project management isn't dependent on the specific type of action item tracker list. Both spreadsheets and more specific project management software can be successful. The key is for employees and management to effectively and consistently monitor and update the tracker.

As the project concludes, project managers like David's management team utilize the action item list to review how successfully each task was completed. They use this data to finetune and streamline procedures and functions for the next project. The ongoing process of monitoring and adjusting procedures to meet organizational goals translates to successfully completing projects.

Action Items Examples

Action item tasks should be very specific. For example, David's management team works to open new branches in different locations. Some examples of action items for his team look like this:

  • Meet with the real estate agent to see office space.
  • Negotiate a lease for office space.
  • Meet to sign the lease.
  • Meet with an architect to design office space.
  • Meet with management to discuss office space design.
  • Finalize office space design.
  • Meet with stakeholders to discuss furniture needs.
  • Meet with a designer to select possible furniture.
  • Meet with stakeholders to finalize furniture selection.
  • Sign contract and order furniture.
  • Call electric company and arrange for electricity to begin on 1/2/22.

As individual action items are planned, the team may organize them on sticky notes on a whiteboard. The notes can be sorted into a logical order.


Writing action items on sticky notes helps the planning process.


The tasks are entered into the action item tracker when finalized. The table below is an example of a spreadsheet-based action item list.

Lesson Summary

Action items are specific, measurable tasks that must be completed as part of a larger project. Creating an action item list helps make a larger project manageable since the work is broken down into individual tasks. Initial planning for any project should include the creation of action item lists that can be utilized throughout the project. The action item list can be used to debrief and evaluate success at the end of the project. Reviewing prior action item lists helps plan for future similar projects. Effective action items should be very specific instructions with all necessary information. For example, when planning a meeting, the action item could read: Email Agenda for 1/28 meeting to all attendees by 3 PM 1/2. A single action item for planning a conference or other large event would ask an assigned staff member to Locate a venue for the event on March 1 by February 1. Effective creation and use of action item lists help ensure the successful completion of complex projects.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are examples of action items?

An example of an action item is a clearly written task that includes instructions for completion. For example, when planning a meeting for January 6th, an action item would instruct "Email meeting agenda to all invited attendees by 3 pm January 5th."

How do you write action items?

Well-written action items are basic instructions for how to complete a task. They need to include due dates along with specific instructions.

What is the definition of an action item?

An action item is a specific, measurable, clearly written task that is part of a larger project. Action items help break down complex projects into manageable activities that can be assigned to specific employees for completion.
